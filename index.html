Privacy Policy

Snapi helps you tidy your camera roll on your own terms. This page explains what the app touches, what stays on your device, and what Snapi doesn’t collect.

Last updated April 18, 2026

At a glance

  • Snapi does not sell your personal information.
  • Your library is not uploaded to Snapi servers—the app works on your device with Apple’s Photos system.
  • Nothing is removed from Photos until you review and confirm.
  • A small amount of app data (like your delete queue) is saved only on your device so Snapi can resume where you left off.

What Snapi does

Snapi is a camera-roll cleaning helper: you swipe through photos and videos, mark what you no longer want, then confirm in review before anything is deleted. You can also share the current item using Apple’s share sheet when you choose to.

Photo library

Snapi needs permission to read and change your photo library so it can show your items, estimate how much space marked items might free up, and delete only after you confirm. If you use Limited Access, only the photos and videos you pick in Apple’s chooser are available to the app—same as any iOS app using Photos.

iCloud and the internet

Some items may live in iCloud Photos. In those cases, Apple’s frameworks may download data over the network so Snapi can show full-quality previews, share, or complete a delete. That happens between your device and Apple’s services, based on your iCloud and device settings—not through servers we run for Snapi.

What Snapi stores on your device

Snapi saves lightweight preferences and session data locally (for example with UserDefaults), including:

  • Which items you’ve marked for deletion, until you confirm or remove them from the list.
  • Your place in the deck and recent swipe history so Undo can work.
  • Your chosen browse mode (for example library, recents, or shuffle).

That information stays on your iPhone or iPad unless you delete the app or clear its data in Settings.

Sharing

When you tap Share, Snapi exports a temporary copy and hands it to Apple’s share sheet. Where it goes next (Messages, Mail, another app) is up to you; Snapi does not control those services.

Other software in the app

Snapi may include small open-source libraries (such as Lottie) only to play on-screen animations. They are not used to track you or send your photos anywhere.

Analytics and ads

The app does not include third-party analytics, crash reporters, or advertising SDKs, and it does not send your library to Snapi-operated backends. If that ever changes, this page will be updated.

Kids

Snapi isn’t aimed at children. Photo libraries can include private material; on shared devices, adults should decide whether the app is appropriate.

Your controls

  • Change or revoke Photos access anytime in Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos.
  • Uninstalling the app removes its local data, subject to how iOS handles app removal.

Updates

This policy may be updated as Snapi evolves. When it is, the date at the top will change. If the law where you live requires more (for example specific disclosures or a contact process), those requirements apply in addition to what’s described here.
