Privacy Policy

Snapi · Last updated: April 18, 2026

This policy describes how Snapi (“the app”) handles information when you use it. Snapi is a utility for browsing your photo library, marking items for removal, and confirming deletions. The app is designed so your photos and videos are processed on your device using Apple’s system frameworks; the app does not operate Snapi-owned servers that receive your library for processing.

Summary

  • Snapi does not sell your personal information.
  • The app does not include in-app analytics, advertising, or user accounts in the current version.
  • Your media stays in Apple Photos unless you use Share (you choose the destination) or confirm a delete in the app.
  • A small amount of app state is stored locally on your device (for example, your delete queue and browsing position).
  • Apple’s Photos and iCloud Photos may download or sync content according to your device settings; that happens through Apple’s systems, not through servers operated for Snapi.

Information the app accesses on your device

Photo library (Photos)

Snapi requests access to your photo library so it can display photos and videos, estimate storage impact, let you mark items for deletion, and delete items when you confirm. If you grant limited access, only the items you select in Apple’s picker are visible to the app, consistent with iOS behavior.

Deleting items uses Apple’s photo library APIs. Depending on your iCloud Photos settings, removed items may be removed from iCloud and your devices in line with how the Photos app normally behaves.

iCloud and network use (Apple Photos only)

For some assets—especially iCloud-backed photos or videos—the app may allow Apple’s Photos frameworks to retrieve full-quality data over the network when needed (for example, to preview, measure size more accurately, export for sharing, or complete a delete). That traffic is between your device and Apple’s services, subject to Apple’s privacy practices and your iCloud settings.

Information stored by the app locally

Snapi keeps lightweight data on your device using standard iOS storage (for example, UserDefaults) so the app can work across launches. This includes:

  • Identifiers for items you marked for deletion (pending until you confirm or clear them).
  • Browsing session data such as your current position in the deck and recent swipe actions (to support undo and resume).
  • Your selected browse mode / filter preference for the deck.

This data is not transmitted to Snapi as part of the app’s normal operation. It remains on your device unless you remove the app or clear the app’s data through iOS settings.

Sharing

If you use Share, Snapi prepares a temporary copy of the selected item and presents Apple’s share sheet. Anything sent afterward is between you and the app or service you choose (Messages, Mail, AirDrop, Files, third-party apps, etc.). Snapi does not control those destinations.

Third-party software

The app may include embedded open-source components used to render animations (for example, Lottie). Those libraries run on your device to display UI; they are not used by Snapi to collect personal data for analytics or advertising in the current app version.

Analytics, tracking, and advertising

The current Snapi codebase does not implement third-party analytics, crash reporting, or advertising SDKs, and does not send library contents to Snapi-operated backends for processing. If that changes in a future version, this policy will be updated and, where required, appropriate consent or permissions will be requested.

Children

Snapi is not directed at children. The app processes the user’s photo library, which may include sensitive content. Parents and guardians should supervise use on shared devices.

Your choices and controls

  • You can grant, limit, or revoke Photos access in iOS Settings.
  • You can uninstall the app; local app data is removed with the app subject to iOS behavior.
  • Deletes you confirm are applied to the library through Apple’s APIs; use the Photos app or your backups if you need recovery options outside the app.

Legal rights

Depending on where you live, privacy laws may give you rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing of personal information, or to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Because Snapi is designed to process your library on-device, much of the information involved never leaves your device as part of Snapi’s own service. Information you voluntarily send through Apple (for example via App Store or system feedback channels) is handled under Apple’s policies and your account settings with Apple.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when that happens. Continued use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy, unless applicable law requires a different approach.

This policy describes the Snapi app’s privacy practices based on its implementation as of the date shown. It is not legal advice. Have counsel review it if you need jurisdiction-specific terms (for example GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA disclosures, or a data processing agreement).
